# EducatedTraveler Community Interest Survey — Report

**Survey period:** Dec 22, 2025 – Feb 18, 2026
**Respondents:** 22
**Report generated:** April 8, 2026

---

## 1. Executive Summary

22 respondents completed the EducatedTraveler Community Interest Survey. The data reveals a predominantly 25-34 year-old, internationally mobile, employed audience with moderate disposable income ($50K-$100K). **39% express strong purchase intent** ("very" or "extremely interested"), with Adventure and Culinary journeys dominating demand. The ideal entry product is a **7-14 day program in the $3,000-$5,000 range** with payment plans. Community features (pre-trip cohort bonding, small groups) are a genuine differentiator. Price and time off work are the two biggest barriers. NFT/blockchain credentials generate more skepticism than excitement.

### Analysis

**Price + time off account for 74% of all barrier mentions.** These are structural, not preference-based — they require product-level solutions:
- **Price:** Payment plans, shorter/cheaper entry programs, early-bird pricing
- **Time off:** 7-14 day programs, weekend/holiday scheduling, corporate sabbatical partnerships
- **Strangers:** Pre-trip cohort bonding solves this
- **No cert needed:** Offer "experience-only" tracks alongside certification paths

## 12. Open-Ended Feedback

### What Excites Most

- "One platform offering everything is convenient"
- "Being immersed in the culture and learning new skills — not just a certificate"
- "The mix of learning and travel"
- "Having a clear goal (certificate) and an immersive experience all planned"
- "Opportunity to have new experiences organised with like-minded people"
- "Really cool idea to get certifications in different parts of the world"
- "I get to learn a skill AND have an immersive experience"

**Theme: convenience + meaning.** People want curated, meaningful skill acquisition without the planning headache.

### What Concerns Most

- "Cost and timing" (repeated)
- "Non-flexible dates"
- "Maybe the people — group dynamic"
- "Not big fan of organised tours — would need to be boutique and off-the-beaten-track"
- "I could book it myself cheaper" (DIY competitor threat)
- "My personal problem is money and time"
- "Would like to know the people more in advance"

**Theme: price sensitivity + fear of generic tourism.** The "I could do it cheaper myself" objection is the existential threat — the value-add of curation, community, and certification quality must be crystal clear.

### Suggested Missing Programs

- Dance / aesthetic treatments
- Horse riding
- Masseuse courses
- Fishing
- Knife making
- Skate classes
- Cooking/life experience immersions

**Pattern: hands-on craft/artisan experiences.** These suggestions lean toward traditional skills and physical crafts — worth exploring for future product lines.

## 13. Strategic Recommendations

### Launch Product
1. **Lead with 7-14 day programs at $1,500-$4,500** — Bali Surf, Burgundy Wine, Costa Rica Spanish
2. **Offer payment plans from day one** — 3-6 month installments, no negotiation
3. **Cap groups at 8-12** — this is a core value proposition, not a nice-to-have

### Pricing Strategy
4. **Anchor at $3,000-$4,999 for 21-day programs** — the data ceiling
5. **Create a "taster" tier** at $1,500-$2,500 (7-day programs) to lower the entry barrier
6. **Money-back guarantee** on first booking — removes risk for fence-sitters

### Community
7. **Pre-trip cohort bonding is mandatory** — WhatsApp group + 2-3 video calls, 60-90 days before departure
8. **"Bring a friend" incentive** — referral discount or +1 pricing
9. **Lightweight alumni network** — WhatsApp groups by cohort, occasional virtual events

### Marketing
10. **Word-of-mouth first** — referral program, ambassador partnerships, alumni testimonials
11. **Content-heavy website** — this audience researches extensively online
12. **Target career transitioners** as early adopters — they have time, motivation, and are actively seeking transformation

### Deprioritize
13. **NFT/blockchain credentials** — save for later, if ever
14. **Heavy saga/stacking marketing** — let it grow organically from satisfied first-timers
15. **Programs above $7,000** at launch — the audience isn't there yet
