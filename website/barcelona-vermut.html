Your host

The five days

Loose on purpose.

Day 1 · Sun arrival vermut

First vermut, then a long lunch

We meet at a bodega in Gràcia at 1pm — vermut on tap, olives, the regulars don't look up. Walk to Christine's friend's flat for a long lunch on the terrace. You'll meet two of her people. They'll remember your name by Thursday.

Ends loosely. Free evening. Pace yourself.

Day 2 · Mon beach beers

Train out, swim in, beers between

30-minute train to a cove most tourists don't reach. Swim. Lunch with our feet in the sand. Plastic-cup beers from a kiosk. An ice cream on the walk back. If the wind's right, we go to the second cove.

Bring a swimsuit + sunscreen. Towel provided.

Day 3 · Tue Penny Banger

The Penny Banger night

A dive bar in Poble-sec — €2 vermuts, a piano nobody plays well, and the best bombas in the city. We get there at 7. We leave when we leave. The owner is named Manolo. He will, with very little encouragement, sing.

Optional. Skip with zero judgment if Tuesday calls for an early one.

Day 4 · Wed opening night

Gallery, then somewhere weirder

An opening at a small gallery in Raval — the kind with cheap cava, free zines, and someone you'll want to keep talking to. Christine knows the door. After, we follow the night where it goes — usually a flat with a roof, sometimes an after-hours bar, occasionally a karaoke room.

Free to bail at any point. The map will get you home.

Day 5 · Thu sweet ending

Slow morning, ice cream, the bench

A long café morning. Three pastry stops Christine actually eats at — a turrón counter from 1908, a corner pâtisserie, and a tiny xocolateria. Ice cream optional but encouraged. We end at the bench in Parc de la Ciutadella where the 4pm light is the reason. You leave with a hand-drawn map of the city you didn't see this trip — for next time.

Wraps by 1pm. Plenty of time for evening flights.

Things people ask

Honest answers.

Do I need Spanish or Catalan?

No. Christine handles every conversation. You'll pick up enough by Wednesday to order your own vermut, which is the only Spanish that matters.

What if I don't drink?

Half the trip is walks, swims, pastry, conversation — vermut is the rhythm, not the requirement. Tell Christine when you book; she'll line up alternatives at every stop.

Is this a tour?

No. It's eight people moving with one local who knows everyone. The itinerary is loose. The point is the rhythm, not the schedule.

What's Christine actually like?

Funny, curious, kind, will keep you out one drink longer than you planned. The friend you'd want when something goes wrong, and especially when it doesn't.

Can I bring a partner?

Yes. Each books their own seat. Apartments have configurations for couples; just write us before checkout.
