Behavioral Science · 11 levers

SHOTTON × ET
Focused Offer
Lead 7–21d certification. Fewer claims = higher credibility.
Price Relativity
Compare to certification programs · not tours · not vacations.
Effort Signals Quality
Show instructor rigor, prep requirements, the 5 AM Toyosu walk.
Distinctiveness
Cinematic visuals · "at the source" · Apple-glass aesthetic.
Scarcity
8–12 seats · fixed dates · waitlist priority. Must be true.
Credible Messenger
Masters · alumni · certifying bodies. Not influencers.
Loss Framing
"Don't waste years before you earn the credential." Respectful.
Concrete Language
"Get certified in 7 days at Koh Tao" beats "transform your life".
Costly Signaling
Show what you sacrificed to build this. 4× trust uplift.
Specificity
"6-stool counter in Tsukiji" closes. "World's best sushi" doesn't.
Unity (Cialdini 7th)
Shared identity > liking. "For people who cook with their hands."

› The Decision Filter · 5 questions

Every new opportunity goes through these in order. First "no" kills it.

1. Does this directly help Sushi Mastery ship full and proud on Sep 21?

2. Can a 70-hr-yacht-week founder execute in <2 hrs/week?

3. Does it pass Daemon non-negotiables + partnership filter?

4. Does it produce a compounding asset (sales page, testimonial reel, evergreen post)?

5. Will I be proud when alum #1 references it in their wedding speech?
