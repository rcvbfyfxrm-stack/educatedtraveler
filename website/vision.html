Tokyo, Japan • 30 Days

Sushi Mastery

Transform from enthusiast to certified sushi chef under the guidance of a Michelin-recognized master itamae.

At 4:47 AM, while Tokyo still sleeps, you'll walk through the gates of Toyosu Market. The air is cold, sharp with the scent of the sea. This is where your transformation begins—not as a tourist, but as an apprentice to a craft perfected over centuries.

Your Master

Master Itamae Takeshi Yamamoto

30+ Years Experience • Former Head Chef, Sukiyabashi Jiro Affiliate

Yamamoto-san trained for 12 years before touching his first fish. His philosophy: sushi is 80% rice, 20% topping, and 100% respect. He has personally trained 23 chefs who now run their own establishments across Japan, New York, and London. His quiet intensity and exacting standards will push you beyond what you thought possible.

Your Journey

Week 1: The Foundation

Shari (Rice) Mastery

You'll wash rice until your hands memorize the motion. Learn the 23-step process to perfect sushi rice. By week's end, you'll understand why masters say "sushi is rice."

Week 2: The Blade

Knife Work & Fish Preparation

Your knife becomes an extension of your hand. Master the five essential cuts. Learn to read fish—its freshness, origin, optimal preparation. Daily sessions at Toyosu with the masters.

Week 3: The Art

Nigiri Technique

Two movements, three seconds, one perfect piece. The hand pressure, the rice count, the marriage of fish and shari. You'll make 500 pieces before the first one satisfies Yamamoto-san.

Week 4: The Service

Full Omakase (8-12 Courses)

You'll serve a complete omakase to real guests. Reading the room, pacing each course, the choreography of hospitality. Your certification dinner—friends and family invited.

Places You'll Experience

Toyosu Fish Market

Daily 5AM visits. Watch the famous tuna auctions. Build relationships with vendors who've supplied Tokyo's best for generations.

Private Sushiya Kitchen

Train in a real 8-seat omakase restaurant. Learn the intimacy of counter service.

Sake Breweries of Niigata

Day trip to Japan's sake heartland. Learn pairing. Return with bottles for your certification dinner.

Knife Forges of Sakai

Visit the 600-year-old bladesmithing capital. Select your professional knife set—yours to keep forever.

Sacred Yoga Pilgrimage

Walk the path where yoga was born. Train on the banks of the Ganges. Conclude with Tibetan teachings and potential blessing from the Dalai Lama.

The Ganges moves past you in the pre-dawn darkness. Incense rises from a thousand temples. You fold into your first Surya Namaskar as the sun breaks over the Himalayas. This isn't a yoga retreat—it's a pilgrimage to where it all began.

Your Guide

Guru Ananda Devi

E-RYT 500 • 25 Years Teaching • Born in Rishikesh

Ananda grew up watching her grandmother practice on the ghats of Rishikesh. She trained under three masters across India before teaching at ashrams in Mysore, Pune, and her home city. Her approach weaves traditional Hatha and Ashtanga with the philosophy that made yoga a path to liberation—not just flexibility. She has guided over 2,000 students to their RYT-200 certification.

The Pilgrimage Route

Delhi

Days 1-3 • Arrival & Acclimatization

Land in India's vibrant capital. Settle into our heritage hotel. Begin morning practice. Walk through Old Delhi's ancient lanes. Prepare your mind and body for the journey ahead.

Rishikesh

Days 4-17 • Core 200-Hour Training

The yoga capital of the world. Train at a traditional ashram overlooking the Ganges. 6AM and 4PM practice. Study asana, pranayama, meditation, anatomy, philosophy. Attend the nightly Ganga Aarti ceremony. This is where Beatles came seeking enlightenment.

Dharamsala

Days 18-24 • Tibetan Teachings

Home of the Dalai Lama and Tibetan government-in-exile. Study Buddhist philosophy with resident monks. Practice in Himalayan mountain air. Attend a public teaching—potential audience with His Holiness depending on schedule.

Delhi

Days 25-27 • Integration & Ceremony

Return transformed. Final teaching practice. Certification ceremony. Farewell dinner with your cohort—bonds forged for life. Departure with your Yoga Alliance credentials.

Sacred Experiences

Ganga Aarti at Triveni Ghat

Nightly fire ceremony on the Ganges. Thousands of flames float downstream. Ancient chants fill the air. Witness devotion that has continued unbroken for millennia.

Beatles Ashram

Meditate where John, Paul, George, and Ringo wrote the White Album. Now reclaimed by the jungle—a spiritual time capsule.

Dalai Lama Temple Complex

Morning kora (circumambulation) with Tibetan pilgrims. Butter tea with monks. Teachings on compassion in the temple where he lives.

Himalayan Sunrise Practice

Practice asana as the sun rises over snow-capped peaks. At 6,000 feet, the air is pure, the silence complete.

Your Captain

Captain James Whitmore

RYA Yachtmaster Ocean • 40,000+ NM Logged • Former ARC Rally Skipper

James crossed his first ocean at 22 and never looked back. He's skippered Atlantic crossings for the ARC rally, delivered yachts from Cape Town to the Caribbean, and trained over 300 sailors to RYA certification. His calm in storms is legendary—because he's seen everything the ocean can throw. He teaches through trust: you will helm in conditions that scare you, and you will emerge confident.

The Passage

Gibraltar

Days 1-5 • Safety, Systems, Seamanship

Where the Mediterranean meets the Atlantic. Learn the boat inside-out. Safety drills until they're muscle memory. First day sails in the strait—one of the world's busiest shipping lanes.

Portuguese Coast

Days 6-14 • Coastal Navigation

Your first overnight passages. Lagos, Sagres (where Henry the Navigator planned his expeditions), Lisbon. Learn to read weather windows. Night watches begin—3 AM helm with only stars for company.

Open Atlantic

Days 15-18 • Ocean Passage

600 nautical miles to Madeira. No land for 4 days. This is where sailors are made. Celestial navigation. Watch rotations. Self-reliance. You'll see why ancient mariners called this the Wine Dark Sea.

Canary Islands

Days 19-21+ • Arrival & Certification

Tenerife, Gran Canaria, Lanzarote. Island hopping, night sails, final assessments. Your certification exam. Then celebration—you've crossed an ocean under your own command.

What You'll Master

Celestial Navigation

Navigate by sun, moon, and stars. When GPS fails 500 miles offshore, you'll know exactly where you are using a sextant and centuries-old mathematics.

Weather Routing

Read GRIB files. Understand pressure systems. Time your passages with weather windows. The difference between a pleasant sail and a survival situation.

Night Watch Command

Solo helm responsibility while your crew sleeps. Collision avoidance. Navigation lights. The profound solitude and alertness of commanding through darkness.

Heavy Weather Sailing

Reefing in 30 knots. Heaving-to in storms. When to run and when to stand. You'll train for conditions you hope to never see—and know what to do if you do.
