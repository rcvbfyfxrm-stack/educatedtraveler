Where it is taught — hand-verified

Schools in Le Sentier, Vallée de Joux

  • WOSTEP (Watchmakers of Switzerland Training and Educational Program)
    WOSTEP Watchmaker Program (3000-hour)
    WOSTEP's flagship Watchmaker Program is a 22-month full-time course at its Neuchâtel school, the classic 3000-hour curriculum recognised across the Swiss industry.
    https://www.wostep.ch/en/training/watchmaker-program
  • École Technique de la Vallée de Joux (ETVJ)
    CFC Horloger·ère (full-time/dual federal watchmaking apprenticeship)
    A technical school in Le Sentier founded in 1901 in the Vallée de Joux watchmaking valley that trains watchmakers, micromechanics, and jeweller-goldsmiths through multi-year federal vocational diplomas.
    https://www.etvj.ch/
  • WOSTEP (Neuchâtel)
    Watchmaker I+II+III (22-month full-time program)
    An independent watchmaking training foundation in Neuchâtel established in 1966 whose 22-month full-time Watchmaker program certifies students in traditional fine-watch repair and craftsmanship.
    https://www.wostep.ch/en
  • CIFOM École Technique (Le Locle)
    CFC Horloger / watchmaking initial and continuing training
    The technical school of Le Locle, heir to the 1868 Le Locle watchmaking school, offering full-time and dual federal watchmaking diplomas plus a modular adult course toward a watch-fitter or watchmaker certificate.
    https://www.cifom.ch/
