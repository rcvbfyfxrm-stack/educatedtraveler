Where it is taught — hand-verified

Schools in Jerez de la Frontera

  • Fundación Cristina Heeren de Arte Flamenco
    Annual Professional Flamenco Course (Dance / Cante / Guitar)
    A nine-month professional program in the Triana neighbourhood of Seville, taught across a 1,500 m2 school with nine studios, a theatre and a recording studio.
    https://www.flamencoheeren.com/en/homepage/
  • Centro Andaluz de Documentación del Flamenco
    A public flamenco documentation and research center created in 1987, housed in the Palacio Pemartín in Jerez's Santiago neighborhood, holding archives, recordings, and a graphic collection, functioning as a study resource rather than a hands-on dance school.
    https://www.centroandaluzdeflamenco.es/
  • Centro de Baile Jerez
    Encuentros Internacionales de Baile Flamenco (summer intensives)
    A long-running Jerez flamenco dance academy whose summer Encuentros Internacionales run multi-week intensive courses with maestros such as Eva Yerbabuena and Manuel Liñán, alongside intensives held during the Festival de Jerez.
    https://www.academiadebailejerez.com/
  • Peña Flamenca Tío José de Paula
    A flamenco peña in Jerez's Santiago neighborhood that hosts regular live performances and Thursday cante (flamenco singing) instruction in November, rather than a structured multi-day immersive program.
    https://www.facebook.com/PFtiojosedepaula/
