Where it is taught — hand-verified

Schools in Tokyo / Mt. Fuji (Kawaguchiko)

  • MI-LAB (Mokuhanga Innovation Laboratory) Artist-in-Residence
    Basic Training Program (BPA / BPB / BPC)
    A residential mokuhanga (Japanese water-based woodblock) artist-in-residence run by MI-LAB.
    https://endeavor.or.jp/mi-lab/air/open-call-for-2026/
  • MI-LAB (Mokuhanga Innovation Laboratory)
    Mokuhanga Artist-in-Residence Program
    MI-LAB is a water-based woodblock (mokuhanga) artist-in-residence program at a residential studio beside Lake Kawaguchi at the foot of Mt. Fuji, running multi-week residencies that teach traditional Japanese woodblock printmaking from introductory to advanced level.
    https://endeavor.or.jp/mi-lab/aboutus/
  • The Adachi Institute of Woodcut Prints
    Adachi Foundation woodblock-printing artisan training program
    The Adachi Institute, founded in 1928 in Tokyo, reproduces ukiyo-e using traditional carving and printing techniques and, through its Adachi Foundation, runs a training program for aspiring woodcut-print artisans alongside periodic public demonstrations and workshops.
    https://www.adachi-hanga.com/en/
  • Mokuhankan (Asakusa)
    Woodblock Print Party (hands-on printing workshop)
    Mokuhankan is woodblock printmaker David Bull's Asakusa workshop and shop in Tokyo, offering hands-on Print Party sessions where visitors ink pre-carved blocks and pull their own prints with a baren.
    https://mokuhankan.com/
