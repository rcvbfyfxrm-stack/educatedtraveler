A

Arnaud

Founder · I check every place myself

I left school at 18 to find out what the world was really about, and I've been crossing it by sea and by land ever since — fifteen years cooking for a living, most of them at sea, learning from the masters I met along the way. Here is some of it.

I · The beginning

So I went.

Everyone expected university and a career. I wanted to learn by doing — not watch the world on a screen. So I left.

II · The kitchens

Every kitchen a classroom.

Michelin kitchens in France, street stalls in Bangkok — most of my years cooking were at sea. Every chef a teacher.

III · The sea

Fifty thousand nautical miles.

Learned to sail the Siam sea, raced off Sydney, crossed oceans. The sea cares about judgment, not a résumé.

IV · The depths

The deeper I went, the quieter it got.

Freediving Mexican cenotes and the open blue, sharks in the Galápagos. Fear turned into respect.

V · The heights

Strangers turned into crew.

Annapurna at 4,130 m, passes across the Himalaya. The people on the trail taught me more than any classroom.

VI · The stillness

Handed, at the source.

Breathwork in Bali, Ayurveda in India — and in the Thar desert, the idea for all of this took shape.

I / VI

What EducatedTraveler is: a place, a person, your people.

Every real skill is learned the same three ways — where it's alive, from a master who'll hand it over, beside the handful chasing the same thing. We connect — we don't sell. Earned, not bought.

“I never found my teachers. They found me.” Now I'm building the way in — the map I wish I'd had, and the doors it opens.

Between the weeks I keep the Atlas — a living map of where crafts are most alive, ranked by community, not by who pays. Nobody buys a place on it. And if I wouldn't send a friend, it isn't on the map.
