## Flagship Adventures — Culinary

### 1. Sushi Mastery — Tokyo
**Duration**: 30 days | **Tier**: Mastery | **XP**: 450

Learn from a master itamae with 30+ years at a Michelin-recognized sushiya. From 5 AM Toyosu Market visits to omakase service. You don't just learn sushi — you learn the Japanese philosophy of shokunin (craftsman's spirit).

**Curriculum**:
- Week 1: Rice (shari) mastery — washing, soaking, cooking, seasoning. The foundation of everything.
- Week 2: Knife work (hocho) and fish preparation — filleting, aging (jukusei), seasonal selection (shun)
- Week 3: Nigiri technique — hand-forming, wasabi placement, pressure control, speed
- Week 4: Full omakase service (8-12 courses) — menu composition, guest reading, presentation, sake pairing

**Certification**: Professional Sushi Chef
**Cohort Size**: 8-10
**Elements**: city, temple
**Desires**: certification, career, rare
**Included**: Instruction, professional knife set (yours to keep), accommodation, breakfast, Toyosu Market visits, sake brewery tour, cultural excursions (Tsukiji outer market, Kappabashi kitchen street)

**Partner targets**: Tokyo Sushi Academy (⚪ in tracker), Hattori Nutrition College, Private itamae via Japan Culinary Arts. Evaluation file exists: `partners/evaluations/tokyo-sushi-academy-hiroki-ishii.md`

### 2. French Pastry — Paris
**Duration**: 30 days | **Tier**: Mastery | **XP**: 450

Paris is where pastry became an art form. Train in the city that invented the croissant, the éclair, the macaron, and the mille-feuille — under chefs who carry the lineage of Escoffier, Lenôtre, and Hermé. This is precision, repetition, and the pursuit of perfection in butter, sugar, and flour.

**Curriculum**:
- Week 1: Foundations — Pâte brisée, sablée, sucrée. Crème pâtissière and its derivatives. Choux (éclairs, profiteroles, Paris-Brest). Tempering. Mise en place discipline at professional speed.
- Week 2: Viennoiserie & Bread — Croissant lamination (détrempe, tourage). Pain au chocolat. Brioche. Baguette. Kouign-amann. Understanding fermentation, hydration, oven behaviour.
- Week 3: Entremets & Chocolate — Multi-component entremets (mousse, insert, glaze, décor). Chocolate tempering and moulding. Macarons. Tarte au citron. Opera cake.
- Week 4: Mastery & Service — Petits fours. Sugar work (pulled, blown). Plated desserts. Boutique window display. Final exam: produce a full pâtisserie vitrine (12+ items) judged by a MOF (Meilleur Ouvrier de France).

**Certification**: Professional Pastry Arts (Le Cordon Bleu)
**Cohort Size**: 8-12
**Elements**: city
**Desires**: certification, career, stories
**Included**: Instruction, all ingredients and equipment, accommodation (Marais apartment), daily pastry breakfast, Paris market tours (Rue Montorgueil, Marché d'Aligre), visits to 3 legendary pâtisseries, Rungis wholesale market visit

**Partner targets**: Le Cordon Bleu Paris (flagship campus), École Ducasse (Alain Ducasse's school), Ferrandi Paris (public, elite reputation). No France country file yet — needs creation.

### 3. Avant-Garde Cuisine — Barcelona
**Duration**: 21 days | **Tier**: Foundation | **XP**: 350

Barcelona is ground zero for the culinary revolution that changed fine dining forever. Ferran Adrià's legacy lives in every kitchen here — from the molecular to the hyper-local, from foam to fermentation. Train with chefs who came up through elBulli, Tickets, Disfrutar, and the Roca brothers. You learn where food becomes idea.

**Curriculum**:
- Week 1: Foundations of Innovation — La Boqueria immersion. Catalan base techniques (sofregit, picada, allioli) as a launchpad. Spherification, gelification, emulsification. Hydrocolloids (agar, gellan, xanthan). Introduction to the Adrià method: concept → technique → dish.
- Week 2: Technique Lab — Sous-vide precision. Fermentation (lacto, koji, garum). Smoke and encapsulation. Texture contrasts (hot/cold, crispy/liquid). Deconstruction: taking a classic (tortilla española, pa amb tomàquet) and reimagining it. Daily lab sessions.
- Week 3: Creation & Service — Each student develops 3 original dishes with full technical rationale. Plating as visual language. Multi-course tasting menu design. Final dinner: 8-course avant-garde menu for 25 guests at a Barcelona venue.

**Certification**: Advanced Culinary Innovation (Basque Culinary Center network / Worldchefs-recognized)
**Cohort Size**: 8-10
**Elements**: city, ocean
**Desires**: certification, career, rare
**Included**: Instruction, lab equipment and ingredients, accommodation (Eixample apartment), daily lunch, 3 avant-garde restaurant dinners (study visits), La Boqueria guided tours, Penedès cava tasting, Empordà coastal excursion

**Partner targets**: Basque Culinary Center (⚪ in tracker — also has Barcelona connections), Mimo San Sebastián/Barcelona. Research needed: elBulli Foundation (educational arm), Disfrutar team (Oriol Castro, Eduard Xatruch — ex-elBulli). Fundació Alícia (food science lab in Manresa, near Barcelona).

### 4. New Basque Cuisine — San Sebastián
**Duration**: 21 days | **Tier**: Foundation | **XP**: 350

The Basque Country has more Michelin stars per capita than anywhere on Earth. But the real magic isn't in fine dining — it's in the txokos (private cooking clubs), the asadores (grill houses), and the pintxos bars of Donostia's Old Town. Learn both: the deep tradition and the boundary-pushing creativity that defines New Basque Cuisine.

**Curriculum**:
- Week 1: Pintxos & Tradition — San Sebastián Old Town immersion. Pintxo architecture (base, topping, binding). Salt cod (bacalao) 5 ways. Txakoli wine. Marmitako. Txoko cooking club sessions with local members.
- Week 2: Fire & Sea — Asador grilling (whole turbot, txuleta steaks over charcoal). Market sourcing at La Bretxa. Spider crab (txangurro). Idiazábal cheese. Cider house (sagardotegia) visit. Basque seafood stew.
- Week 3: Modern Basque — Avant-garde techniques from the Arzak/Mugaritz lineage. Fermentation, smoke, texture manipulation. Desserts (gâteau basque, pantxineta). Final dinner: 10-course pintxos + plated menu for 30 guests.

**Certification**: Basque Culinary Arts (Basque Culinary Center-affiliated)
**Cohort Size**: 8-10
**Elements**: ocean, city
**Desires**: certification, career, rare
**Included**: Instruction, accommodation (San Sebastián apartment), daily pintxo crawls, txoko membership access, cider house visit, market days, Rioja wine excursion, all course ingredients

**Partner targets**: Basque Culinary Center (⚪ in tracker — top priority), Arzak (stage opportunities), Mugaritz (Andoni Aduriz — R&D access), Asador Etxebarri (Victor Arguinzoniz — fire mastery), Mimo San Sebastián (pintxos specialty). All listed in Spain country file.

### 5. Terroir Cooking — Tuscany
**Duration**: 30 days | **Tier**: Mastery | **XP**: 450

Tuscany is where cooking means the land. No technique compensates for a bad tomato. This immersion teaches you to cook by understanding soil, seasons, producers, and the ruthless simplicity of Italian cuisine — where three ingredients must be perfect or the dish fails. You cook in farmhouse kitchens, harvest with farmers, and eat at tables where the same family has cooked for generations.

**Curriculum**:
- Week 1: The Farmhouse Kitchen — Fresh pasta (pici, pappardelle, ravioli) by hand with sfogline. Ribollita, panzanella, pappa al pomodoro — cucina povera (poor cooking that became iconic). Bread without salt (pane toscano) and why. Olive harvest and oil pressing (seasonal). Bistecca alla fiorentina.
- Week 2: Producers & Preserving — Parmigiano-Reggiano dairy visit (Emilia-Romagna day trip). Pecorino di Pienza. Chianina beef farms. Salumi and curing (finocchiona, lardo di Colonnata). Conserva: sun-dried tomatoes, sott'olio, passata. Truffle hunting in San Miniato (seasonal).
- Week 3: Wine & Land — Chianti Classico immersion: vineyard work, harvest understanding, cooking with wine. Brunello di Montalcino visit. Vin Santo and cantucci. Wild boar (cinghiale) ragù. Herb and forage walks. Cooking over wood fire.
- Week 4: The Table — Risotto, soups, whole-animal nose-to-tail. Dolci toscani (castagnaccio, schiacciata con l'uva, zuccotto). Building a multi-course pranzo della domenica (Sunday lunch). Final feast: cohort cooks a full Tuscan harvest dinner for 30 at an agriturismo.

**Certification**: Italian Culinary Arts (Worldchefs-recognized)
**Cohort Size**: 8-12
**Elements**: wild, city
**Desires**: certification, career, stories
**Included**: Instruction, agriturismo accommodation, all meals during training, Parmigiano dairy visit, Chianti vineyard days, truffle hunt (seasonal), olive oil pressing, Montalcino wine tour, final feast at agriturismo

**Partner targets**: Tuscany cooking schools (listed in Italy country file — need specific research). Top candidates: cooking schools in the Chianti/Val d'Orcia area. Also: Antinori (600-year wine dynasty, ⚪), Biondi-Santi (Brunello creators). Need to research: Desinare (Florence, serious Tuscan cooking), La Cucina del Sole (Tuscan farmhouse programs), Divina Cucina (Judy Witts Francini — expat legend in Florence markets).

### 6. Indigenous Cuisine — Oaxaca
**Duration**: 21-28 days | **Tier**: Foundation | **XP**: 300

Oaxaca is the culinary soul of Mexico — seven moles, mezcal, chapulines, tlayudas, and a food culture UNESCO recognized as Intangible Heritage. This isn't restaurant cooking. You learn from abuelas and cocineras tradicionales in village kitchens, milpas, and markets. You grind on metate, roast chiles over comal, and cook mole the way it's been made for centuries — by hand, by smell, by memory.

**Curriculum**:
- Week 1: Ancestral Foundations — Nixtamalización (corn processing from scratch on metate). Tortilla making. Salsa taxonomy (20+ varieties). Dried chiles: identification, toasting, rehydrating. Black beans. Comal technique. Market navigation at Mercado de Abastos and Tlacolula Sunday market.
- Week 2: The Seven Moles — Master 3 of the 7 classic Oaxacan moles (negro, rojo, coloradito) with village cocineras. Chocolate from cacao (pre-Hispanic technique). Tamales oaxaqueños. Memelas, tlayudas, chapulines. Each mole in a different village kitchen.
- Week 3: Fermentation & Fire — Mezcal palenque immersion (earth ovens, stone milling, clay pot distillation). Pulque and tepache. Barbacoa pit cooking. Large-format fiesta techniques. Mole for 50.
- Week 4 (if 28 days): The Feast — Street food module (tlayudas, elotes, tejate). Menu creation blending ancestral and contemporary. Final night: cohort cooks a full Oaxacan feast for the community at a local venue.

**Certification**: CESSA Professional Certificate
**Cohort Size**: 8-10
**Elements**: wild, temple
**Desires**: certification, stories, rare
**Included**: Instruction, village homestay + Oaxaca Centro accommodation, all meals, mezcal palenque visit, cacao workshop, milpa farm visit (corn-bean-squash ancient system), textile/craft village visits, Hierve el Agua excursion, community feast costs

**Add-ons**: Mezcal Sommelier Workshop (+$350), Day of the Dead Cooking (+$250, seasonal), Zapotec Archaeological Tour (+$200), Home Cooking with Abuela (+$150)

**Partner targets**: Seasons of My Heart — Susana Trilling (James Beard winner, 30+ years, priority #1 in Mexico file). Casa de los Sabores — Pilar Cabrera (⚪ in tracker, high priority). Casa Oaxaca — Alejandro Ruiz (Mexico's best Oaxacan restaurant). Zapotec grandmothers in Tlacolula (custom village immersion). Real Minero — Graciela Angeles (mezcal, female maestro, ⚪ in tracker). Already live in `experiences.js` as `indigenous-mexican-oaxaca`.

### 7. Become a Yacht Chef — Antibes
**Duration**: 21-28 days | **Tier**: Foundation-Mastery | **XP**: 400

You can cook. Now learn to cook at sea. This is the only programme that takes you from qualified chef to hire-ready yacht chef in one cohort — STCW, ENG1, Ship's Cook Certificate, and real galley training, all in Antibes, the yachting capital of Europe. You leave with every cert you need, a portfolio captains actually read, and introductions to crew agencies.

**Curriculum**:
- Week 1: STCW Basic Safety Training (MCA-approved, 5 days) — sea survival, firefighting, first aid, personal safety. This is your ticket to work at sea. Delivered by Seascope France.
- Week 2: Yacht Galley Immersion — Galley setup and workflow. Provisioning runs in Antibes, Golfe-Juan, Nice markets. Preference sheet analysis. Crew meals (feeding 12 on a budget, twice a day, every day). Cold storage management. Menu planning for a 7-day charter rotation.
- Week 3: Charter Prep & Fine Dining — Multi-course guest menus. Dietary restrictions (coeliac, keto, halal, allergies — real ones from real preference sheets). Wine service basics. Plating for small plates and formal. Working with the chief stew. Timing service from the galley with no line cook.
- Week 4: Ship's Cook Certificate + Launch — ENG1 medical (30 min, MCA-approved doctor in Antibes). Ship's Cook Certificate assessment (2 days, Ocean Wave). Portfolio review and CV prep. Crew agency introductions. Final dinner: 8-course menu cooked solo in a yacht galley for real guests.

**Certifications**:
- STCW Basic Safety (MCA, valid worldwide)
- ENG1 Seafarer Medical Certificate (2-year validity)
- Ship's Cook Certificate (CISR, required for 10+ crew yachts)
- Food Safety Level 3 (included with Ship's Cook)

**Cohort Size**: 8
**Elements**: ocean, city
**Desires**: certification, career
**Included**: All training fees (STCW + Ship's Cook + ENG1), ingredients for all practical sessions, accommodation (crew house in Antibes), daily lunch, market visits, yacht galley access for training, portfolio photo session, crew agency introduction package

**What makes this different from existing courses**:
- WhitePepper (UK) teaches cooking skills but no certs — you still need STCW and Ship's Cook separately
- Seascope does STCW but no cooking — you're cert-ready but not galley-ready
- Ocean Wave does Ship's Cook assessment but assumes you're already working — no training included
- Get Onboard (Cape Town) is the closest full package but wrong hemisphere for Med season
- **Nobody combines all certs + real galley training + provisioning + crew agency access in one cohort, in the town where the boats actually are**

**Partner targets**:
- STCW: Seascope France, Antibes (MCA-approved, €1,150, multiple dates/month) or Bluewater Antibes
- Ship's Cook Certificate: Ocean Wave Monaco (runs assessments in Antibes, €1,350-1,650)
- ENG1: Dr Bruno Lavagne (Antibes) or Dr Gordon (Cabinet Medical Gordon, Antibes)
- Food Safety Level 3: Ocean Wave Monaco (bundled with Ship's Cook)
- Galley access: Partner with a yacht in Antibes port for real training environment
- Crew agencies: Yachting Partners International (YPI), Bluewater, Dockwalk, The Crew Network

**Your edge**: You've done 40+ charters. You know what captains actually want, what preference sheets really mean, and which ports have the best suppliers. Your Littoralicious provisioning index IS the curriculum for the provisioning module. Your WhatsApp groups (24k messages) are the real intel. No school administrator can teach this.
