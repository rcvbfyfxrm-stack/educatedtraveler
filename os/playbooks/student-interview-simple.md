# Student Interview — Quick Guide
## *10 Essential Questions to Vet Any Instructor*

---

## The 10 Questions That Matter

### 1. Why did you choose this instructor?
*What drew them specifically — reputation, recommendation, unique skill?*

### 2. What was a typical training day like?
*Hours, structure, hands-on vs observation*

### 3. How much personal attention did you get?
*Group size, individual feedback, corrections*

### 4. What makes this instructor different from others?
*Their unique method, philosophy, or expertise*

### 5. Describe a moment you saw their true passion for the craft.
*Story that shows authenticity and dedication*

### 6. What can you do now that you couldn't do before?
*Specific skills gained*

### 7. Was it worth the investment?
*Honest value assessment*

### 8. What could have been better?
*Gaps we could fill*

### 9. Would you recommend them to a serious student?
*Enthusiastic yes, qualified yes, or hesitation?*

### 10. Is this the best training of its kind in the world?
*The ultimate question*

---

## Quick Scoring

After the call, answer:

| Question | Yes/No |
|----------|--------|
| Did the student sound genuinely enthusiastic? | |
| Did they describe real transformation? | |
| Would they do it again? | |
| Would they recommend without hesitation? | |
| Did they say "it changed my life" or similar? | |

**4-5 Yes = Pursue this partner**
**2-3 Yes = Dig deeper**
**0-1 Yes = Pass**

---

## Red Flags (Automatic Pass)

- "It was fine" (no enthusiasm)
- Safety concerns mentioned
- "I wouldn't do it again"
- Couldn't describe what they learned

## Green Flags (Pursue Immediately)

- "Changed my life"
- "Best decision I ever made"
- "I still practice what they taught me"
- "I'm still in touch with the instructor"
