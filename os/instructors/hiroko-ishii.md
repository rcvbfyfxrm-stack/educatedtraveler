## Background
Hiroko Ishii is a Tokyo-based sushi chef and instructor. She studied food science at university in Japan, trained in French cuisine at Le Cordon Bleu Paris, then learned authentic sushi at Tokyo Sushi Academy. From 2009 to 2018, she worked in Switzerland as a sushi chef for a gastronomic catering company and taught sushi at a local cooking school. She founded the Japan Culinary Masters Academy (JCMA) in Tokyo to train international chefs in authentic Japanese sushi preparation.

## Curriculum Detail

### Level 1 — Basic (Weeks 1-2)
**Skills:** Knife handling & cutting, sushi rice (shari), fish fillets (saku), seafood preparation (shrimp, squid), nigiri fundamentals, hosomaki
**Theory:** Sushi culture & history, key ingredients (rice, vinegar, nori, wasabi), knife maintenance & sharpening
**Exam:** Prepare a complete sushi plate — technique, balance, presentation

### Level 2 — Intermediate (Weeks 3-4)
**Skills:** Fish butchery & filleting, futomaki & uramaki, sashimi cutting, sashimi plating & presentation, counter etiquette & service movement
**Theory:** Fish identification, fish seasonality, ingredient quality & selection
**Exam:** Serve custom sushi orders at the counter, simulating a real sushi bar

### Level 3 — Superior (Weeks 5-6)
**Skills:** Delicate fish (anago, hirame), traditional otsumami (bar appetizers), creative nigiri, complete omakase sushi course
**Theory:** Omakase menu structure, course balance & flow, guest experience at the counter, fish market visit
**Exam:** Present and serve a complete omakase course at the sushi counter
