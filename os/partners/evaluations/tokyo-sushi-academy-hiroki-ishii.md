## Questions to Ask Former Students

### Opening — The Decision

1. **Why did you choose Tokyo Sushi Academy and specifically training with Hiroki Ishii?**
   - Was it reputation? Recommendation? Language accessibility?
   - Did you research other sushi schools in Tokyo?

2. **What other sushi training options did you consider?**
   - Hattori Nutrition College? Private apprenticeships?
   - What made Tokyo Sushi Academy stand out?

3. **What were your expectations of learning sushi in Tokyo?**
   - Did you expect the "10 years on rice" traditional approach?
   - What skill level did you hope to achieve?

---

### The Sushi Training Experience

4. **Describe a typical training day with Hiroki-san.**
   - What time did you start? End?
   - How was the day divided (rice, fish prep, knife work, nigiri)?
   - How much hands-on sushi making vs observation?

5. **What was Hiroki-san's teaching style?**
   - Traditional stern Japanese master or more accessible?
   - Did he explain the "why" or expect you to observe and learn?
   - How did he communicate — demonstration, verbal instruction, hands-on correction?

6. **How much personal attention did you receive from Hiroki-san?**
   - How many students in your cohort?
   - Did he correct your technique individually?
   - Could you ask questions freely?

7. **What was the most challenging part of your training?**
   - Rice preparation? Knife skills? Fish handling?
   - How did Hiroki-san help you through difficulties?

8. **What surprised you most about learning sushi in Tokyo?**
   - The intensity? The philosophy? The attention to detail?
   - Anything about Hiroki-san specifically?

---

### Hiroki Ishii — The Instructor

9. **What makes Hiroki Ishii different from other sushi instructors?**
   - His technique? Philosophy? Teaching method?
   - Something only he could teach you?

10. **Can you describe a moment when you saw Hiroki-san's true passion for sushi?**
    - A story that showed his dedication to the craft?
    - How long has he been making sushi? Does it still excite him?

11. **How did Hiroki-san handle your mistakes?**
    - When you cut fish wrong, shaped nigiri poorly, ruined rice?
    - Patient? Critical? Constructive?

12. **Did Hiroki-san share personal stories about his journey as an itamae?**
    - Where he trained? Who his masters were?
    - His philosophy on sushi?

13. **Did you visit Tsukiji/Toyosu market together?**
    - Did Hiroki-san teach you how to select fish?
    - Did you see his relationships with fishmongers?

---

### Sushi-Specific Technical Questions

14. **How much time did you spend on rice preparation?**
    - Did you understand why rice is considered the most important element?
    - Can you now prepare shari (sushi rice) to professional standard?

15. **What knife skills did you develop?**
    - Types of cuts for different fish?
    - Did you receive your own knife set?
    - Could you now fillet fish independently?

16. **How many types of fish did you work with?**
    - Seasonal fish? Specialty cuts?
    - Did you learn to identify quality and freshness?

17. **Did you learn nigiri-zushi technique that you could now execute independently?**
    - The hand movements, rice amount, wasabi placement?
    - Would you serve your nigiri to a discerning guest?

18. **What traditional techniques did Hiroki-san teach that you wouldn't learn elsewhere?**
    - Edomae aging methods (kobujime, zuke)?
    - Secrets passed down from his masters?

---

### Cultural Depth

19. **Did you learn about the philosophy and history of Edomae sushi?**
    - Why Tokyo sushi is different from other regional styles?
    - The aesthetics, seasonality, and presentation principles?

20. **Did the training connect you to the broader Tokyo sushi world?**
    - Visits to famous sushi restaurants?
    - Understanding of the itamae hierarchy and culture?

21. **How did this experience change your appreciation for sushi?**
    - Can you now recognize quality sushi anywhere in the world?
    - Do you understand what separates good from exceptional?

---

### The Transformation

22. **What can you do now that you couldn't do before training with Hiroki-san?**
    - Specific skills: Cut fish? Make proper rice? Shape nigiri?
    - Confidence level working with raw fish?

23. **How has this experience affected your life since returning home?**
    - Do you make sushi regularly?
    - Has it changed your career? Opened a restaurant? Teaching others?

24. **Would you say this was the best sushi training available anywhere in the world?**
    - Why or why not?
    - What would be better?

---

### Value Assessment

25. **What was the total investment (tuition, accommodation, travel, time)?**
    - Was it worth it?
    - What would you have paid knowing what you know now?

26. **What could have made the experience even better?**
    - More time? Different focus? Additional elements?
    - Anything EducatedTraveler could add?

27. **How does this compare to other culinary training you've done?**
    - Cooking schools in other countries?
    - Other Japanese culinary experiences?

---

### Recommendation

28. **Would you recommend training with Hiroki-san to someone serious about sushi?**
    - Enthusiastic yes? Qualified yes? Hesitation?
    - Why specifically?

29. **What type of person would thrive in Hiroki-san's program?**
    - Dedication level required?
    - Prior experience needed?
    - Personality type?

30. **What type of person should NOT train with Hiroki-san?**
    - Who would struggle or not benefit?
    - Any dealbreakers?

31. **On a scale of 1-10, how likely are you to recommend Tokyo Sushi Academy with Hiroki Ishii to a friend interested in sushi?**
    - Score:
    - Why that score?

## Key Questions to Determine World-Class Status

**THE THREE KILLER QUESTIONS:**

1. **"Is there anywhere else in the world you could have learned what Hiroki-san taught you?"**
   - Looking for: "No, only in Tokyo with him"

2. **"Would you trust the sushi you make now to be served at a high-end omakase counter?"**
   - Looking for: Confidence in their skill level

3. **"If you could go back, would you train with Hiroki-san again, or would you choose someone else?"**
   - Looking for: "Absolutely Hiroki-san again"

## Red Flags Specific to Sushi Training

| Red Flag | Concern |
|----------|---------|
| "We didn't work with real fish much" | Tourist experience, not professional training |
| "Class was mostly watching" | Not enough hands-on |
| "There were 20+ students" | Not enough personal attention |
| "We never went to the fish market" | Missing key cultural element |
| "It felt rushed" | Quantity over quality |
| "Language was a real barrier" | Communication problems with Hiroki-san |

---

| Green Flag | Significance |
|------------|--------------|

| "I can taste quality sushi differently now" | Palate transformed |
