# EducatedTraveler Brand Guardrails

This document captures the core brand rules and ethics for EducatedTraveler. Treat this as a checklist for any new copy, UX, or product framing.

## 1. The Three Pillars — Freedom, Adventure, Immersion

Every experience description, every page, every piece of copy must evoke all three:

- **Freedom** — The open sea, the unknown horizon, the life you chose. We sell purpose in extraordinary places, not itineraries.
- **Adventure** — Real stakes, real effort, real reward. Earned exhilaration, not curated comfort.
- **Immersion** — Deep in the craft, the culture, the cohort. Living it, not skimming it.

**The rhythm:** *Surf in Hawaii. Sail the open sea. Cook in Tokyo.* — Specific skill + evocative place or feeling. Never generic. Sailing evokes the open sea and freedom, not a port name. Surfing evokes Hawaii because that's where it was born. Cooking evokes Tokyo because that's where sushi mastery lives.

## 2. Spectrum, Not Random Durations
- Always use the official ladder:
  - 7-21 days -> **Foundation (Get certified)**
  - 30-60 days -> **Mastery (Go deeper)**
  - 90-180 days -> **Saga (Change your life)**
- Do **not** invent extra durations (e.g. "2 years", "several years") that don't match the product spectrum.

## 3. Lead With 7-21 Day Adventures
- Short immersions are the primary entry point.
- Longer options (1-3 months, 3-6 months) are framed as **deeper paths**, not the default.

## 4. Real Certifications, Not Vague Learning
- Anchor offerings on **recognized certifications** (PADI, RYA, Yoga Alliance, WSET, culinary credentials, etc.).
- Emphasize progression and standards, not just "experiences."

## 5. Cohorts, Not Crowds
- Use the word **"cohort"** (not "group" or "class").
- Respect size limits:
  - 8-12 max (6-8 for sailing)
- Avoid any language implying big tour groups or mass tourism.

## 6. Serious Training, Not Easy Hacks
- Never promise "easy" or "effortless" mastery.
- Make it clear these are **serious trainings** with real effort, standards, and accountability.

## 7. Time & Rest Non-Negotiables
- Do not design or describe schedules that violate:
  - **<= 90 minute sitting blocks**
  - **1 rest day per week minimum**
- If in doubt, bias toward more rest and integration time, not less.

## 8. Instructor Quality Is Sacred
- Instructors should be framed as:
  - **5+ years experience**
  - Properly **certified** in their discipline
- Never imply that casual or uncertified leaders are acceptable.

## 9. Voice & Words Discipline
- **Use:**
  - "**Immersion**" (method, feeling, and brand)
  - "**Freedom**" (the promise — choose your life, earn your path)
  - "**Adventure**" (the experience — real effort, real stakes)
  - "**The open sea**" / "**open water**" (for sailing — never pin it to one port)
  - "**Earned**" (not bought, not given)
  - "**Skill adventures at the source**"
  - "**Earned, not bought. Certified at the source.**"
  - "**Choose your depth**"
  - "**Certified**"
  - "**Cohort**"
- **Avoid:**
  - "Transformation" (passive — immersion is what you *do*)
  - "Vacation" / "holiday" (we sell earned adventures, not leisure)
  - Generic "life-changing" claims without context
  - Vague use of "journey"
  - "Luxury" (we are **premium**, not indulgent)
  - "Easy" or "effortless"

## 10. Premium, Not Indulgent
- Aesthetic and language should feel **cinematic, minimal, precise**.
- Think **Apple glass meets Netflix minimal**, not a discount travel agency or flashy luxury resort.

## 11. Respect the Source & the Student
- Emphasize learning **at the source with respect** for local culture, environment, and people.
- Avoid extractive or exploitative framing.
- Honor the effort and commitment students bring; do not trivialize their work.
