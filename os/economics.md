## Pricing Philosophy

**Foundation ($4,500)**:
- Must filter for serious learners
- Yet accessible without trust-fund backing
- Validation: 40% say "expensive but worth it" = price is right

**Pricing Signals**:
- Everyone says "cheap" → raise price
- Everyone says "expensive" → fix messaging
- 40% say "expensive but worth it" → perfect

## Cohort Economics

### Example: Bali Yoga 21-day ($4,500/person)

**At 8 participants (minimum)**:
```
Revenue:        8 × $4,500 = $36,000
Variable Costs: 8 × $2,100 = $16,800
Fixed Costs:                  $8,000
Gross Profit:                $11,200
Margin:                         31%
```

**At 12 participants (maximum)**:
```
Revenue:        12 × $4,500 = $54,000
Variable Costs: 12 × $2,100 = $25,200
Fixed Costs:                   $8,000
Gross Profit:                 $20,800
Margin:                          39%
```

**Breakeven**: 5 participants
